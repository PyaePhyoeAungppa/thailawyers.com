Frequently Asked Questions

Yes, foreigners can own 100% of a company in Thailand depending on whether it is allowed by the Foreign Business Law. International investors may also establish branch offices or Treaty of Amity companies (specifically for American citizens) under certain regulations.
A representative office is suitable if your establishment in Thailand is not for earning income but for activities like reporting business movements, providing advice about goods, inspecting quality and quantity of goods, or disseminating information related to the head office or affiliated companies.
BOI (Board of Investment) promotion is favored because most BOI-promoted companies can be 100% foreign-owned and are allowed more work permits than non-BOI companies. This provides significant business advantages in Thailand.
Many unfamiliar with Thai laws find the BOI registration process complicated. Some law firms charge high fees, but issues often arise involving financial, accounting, and tax matters, which lawyers alone may not cover.
